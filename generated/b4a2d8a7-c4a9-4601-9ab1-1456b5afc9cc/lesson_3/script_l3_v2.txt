Welcome to 'Healthy Eating on a Budget.' Eating nutritious food doesn't have to break the bank. Start by planning your meals for the week. This helps reduce food waste and saves money. Next, make a shopping list based on your meal plan and stick to it. Look for sales and discounts on healthy items, and consider buying in bulk. Frozen fruits and vegetables are often cheaper and just as nutritious as fresh ones. Don't forget to compare unit prices to get the best deal. Incorporate more plant-based proteins, like beans and lentils, which are generally less expensive than meat. Lastly, try cooking at home more often. It’s healthier and more economical than dining out. With these tips, you can fuel your body without overspending. Let’s dive into meal prep in the next video!
